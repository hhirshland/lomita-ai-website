## Part 1 — Shared Foundation

### Positioning thesis

> Every revolution rewires capital.
> We help institutional capital be on the right side of the AI one.

Hyperactive is repositioning to speak to the people who allocate, not just the people who operate. The Industrial Revolution rebuilt how value was produced; the Agricultural Revolution rebuilt how it was stored and traded; the Scientific Revolution rebuilt how it was reasoned about. The AI revolution does all three at once — and the partners institutional capital chooses for this transition will define the next twenty-five years of returns.

The brand has to feel like it belongs in that lineage: heritage in its bearing, contemporary in its toolkit.

### Audience

Primary: fund-of-funds, GP relations teams, IC members at private equity and venture firms, family-office CIOs.

Secondary: GPs of portfolio companies where the LP needs AI literacy delivered top-down.

What they value, in priority order:

1. **Signal over noise.** Short claims, backed by primary evidence.
2. **Peer credibility.** Who else trusts you, in their own words.
3. **Continuity.** A sense that you've seen — or studied — more than one cycle.
4. **Discretion.** No screaming, no gradients, no growth-hacking vocabulary.
5. **Throughput.** When they decide to engage, they want one pre-read and one call, not a six-week sales motion.

### Voice & tone (cross-direction principles)

- **Declarative.** Sentences end. Avoid hedges ("we believe," "we think we can"). State the position, then evidence it.
- **Evidence-backed.** Every adjective earns its place with a number, a name, or a primary source.
- **Free of AI-agency clichés.** No "unlock," "leverage AI," "transform with AI," "supercharge," "next-gen."
- **Aware of the reader's calendar.** A subordinate sentence is a tax on a partner's morning. Trim.
- **Heritage without nostalgia.** We invoke historical revolutions to set scale, not to cosplay.

### Anti-patterns to avoid

- Gradient-mesh "AI sci-fi" backgrounds, glowing neural-net diagrams, robot/circuit clichés
- Generic SaaS minimalism — endless white, sans-serif everything, no point of view
- Sepia for sepia's sake — heritage should feel structural, not Instagram-filtered
- Stock photography of handshakes, glass skyscrapers, or anyone in front of a server rack

### 5-question rubric for the live mocks

1. **Would an IC chair forward this to two colleagues without comment?** If they'd need to caveat it, the visual is the caveat.
2. **Can I read the hero before the next chime on my phone?** Headline-and-subhead should land in under three seconds.
3. **Does the typography belong on a balance sheet, or on a startup deck?** We want the former.
4. **Is the accent color earning its presence?** One accent, doing structural work, beats three accents decorating.
5. **If I removed the word "AI," would the brand still feel modern?** It should.

## Part 2 — The three options on the live styleguide

The live showcase at `/styleguide` lets you compare all three side-by-side. Below is the written companion — what each direction is reaching for, and why.

### Current — "Product Lab" _(baseline, for reference)_

- **Concept:** the in-market design — modular product-lab aesthetic with grid backgrounds, annotation tags, and status dots. Reads as "thoughtful operator."
- **Type:** Inter (body/UI) + JetBrains Mono (annotations, tags, IDs)
- **Color:** ink `#141414`, paper `#FAFAFA`, teal `#0D9373` (CTA), blue `#2563EB` (info), orange `#F06D2D` (energy)
- **Texture:** 40px lab grid, hand-note underlines, tag pills, status dots
- **Voice sample:** "Transform your enterprise for the AI Era."
- **Strength against the audience:** clearly competent, contemporary, not a deck
- **Risk against the audience:** reads as another AI startup; lacks the heritage weight an allocator looks for in a long-term partner
- **Why it's in the comparison:** so every other direction is measured as a delta from what we ship today, not as a delta from a vacuum

### Variant A — "Bureau · Salon"

> Bureau in its quietest register. No imagery. Typography does everything.

The drawing-room edition. The brand that lets a serif headline, a hairline rule, and twenty millimeters of whitespace carry the meaning. For the firm whose authority comes from what is not said.

- **Voice sample:** "Counsel to capital, calibrated for the AI era."
- **Typography:**
  - Display + body: EB Garamond (500 / italic for emphasis)
  - Sans: Inter, used sparingly for UI elements
  - Mono: IBM Plex Mono — classification labels only, 0.24em tracking, all caps
- **Color:**
  - Charcoal `#1A1A1A` (primary text)
  - Ivory `#F6F2EA` (background)
  - Navy `#1B2A4E` (structural accent — links, rules, monogram)
  - Verdigris `#5E7F6E` (rare accent — used once or twice per page)
  - Hairline `#D6CFC0` (0.5px rules)
- **Texture & material cues:** monogram H, 0.5px hairline rules, dossier numbering (`§ 01 · 02 · 03`), classification labels, generous whitespace
- **Component cues:** classification labels (`CONFIDENTIAL · IC PRE-READ`), numbered dossier sections, signature block, hairline-bordered cards
- **Best for:** firms that value composure above all. The brand that doesn't raise its voice at the conference.
- **Risk:** can read static or sleepy. We solve this with sharp data viz and one piece of motion per section, not by adding decoration.

### Variant B — "Bureau · Atelier"

> Bureau with the heritage made visible. Romantic painted landscapes — Hudson River School lineage, Maxfield Parrish atmosphere — frame the writing about AI.

The painted edition. Same heritage thesis as Salon, but stated in oil paint rather than implied through type. Instead of Victorian wood engravings of mills and machines, the imagery is the **pastoral side** of the same revolutions — golden wheat fields, vast painted skies, river valleys with quiet industry at the horizon. The land institutional capital has always represented, looking forward.

**Reference:** [cora.computer](https://cora.computer/) — atmospheric full-bleed paintings with floating UI cards. Same painterly sensibility, different content.

- **Voice sample:** "We belong in the lineage of revolutions that built the modern economy."
- **Typography:**
  - Display + body: EB Garamond (600 weight — slightly heavier than Salon, to hold its own against the paintings)
  - Mono: IBM Plex Mono — plate labels, captions, footnotes
  - Drop caps in sky navy after section breaks
- **Color (harmonized with the paintings):**
  - Night ink `#14202E` (primary text — reads as the deep blue of dusk in the paintings)
  - Cream stock `#F2EAD7` (background — the painted-paper warmth)
  - Sky navy `#2B4A7A` (primary accent — links, drop caps, classification labels)
  - Harvest gold `#B07A2C` (warm accent — used rarely, for emphasis on numbers)
  - Verdigris `#3E5A4C` (structural accent — rules, secondary marks)
  - Mortar `#E5DCC4` (secondary surface)
- **Texture & material cues:**
  - Three painted landscape plates rendered without filters (the paintings already are the texture)
  - Floating "glass" content cards over full-bleed landscape backgrounds in the hero (the Cora signature)
  - Quiet plate labels in mono small caps (`Pastoral · I`, `Long View · II`, `Confluence · III`)
  - Italic serif captions under each plate (no Victorian "Fig." framing)
  - Drop caps in sky navy at the start of major paragraphs
  - Subtle ornamental rule between sections (`~ · ~`)
- **Imagery currently in use** _(AI-generated placeholders in the Hudson River School / Maxfield Parrish vein — to be replaced with commissioned or licensed paintings)_:
  - `Pastoral · I` — Wheat fields at harvest with mountains and painted clouds (masthead)
  - `Long View · II` — Vast sky with luminous cumulus at sunset, thin strip of land (hero, full-bleed)
  - `Confluence · III` — River valley at dusk with a small Victorian mill on the distant horizon (case study)
- **Component cues:** painted plates, italic captions, floating glass cards over landscapes, drop caps, subtle ornamental rules
- **Best for:** firms that want the heritage thesis felt rather than stated — "this brand belongs in the same room as the paintings on our walls." Atmospheric, expansive, not literal.
- **Risk:** if executed half-heartedly, can drift into wedding-stationery / Pinterest territory. We solve this with restraint (three paintings per long page max, never decorative — each one earns its place), sharp typography on top of soft imagery, and contemporary copy.

## Part 3 — What's in the live mock for each direction

Every direction page at `/styleguide/<key>` contains the same nine blocks, in the same order. The blocks are theme-agnostic — they read from CSS variables — so the same vocabulary renders three ways. Atelier additionally swaps in `<PaintedPlate>` elements and a full-bleed landscape hero.

1. **Masthead** — name, voice sample, dateline-style metadata; Atelier adds a featured painted landscape (`Pastoral · I`)
2. **Type specimen** — display, H1, H2, H3, body, small caps, mono — identical copy across all three
3. **Color swatches** — 5–6 swatches with hex, role, and a usage hint
4. **Component samples** — primary/secondary buttons, tags, badges, callouts, dividers, identity mark
5. **Section: Hero** — sample hero treatment; Atelier renders a full-bleed landscape (`Long View · II`) with a floating glass content card over it (the Cora pattern)
6. **Section: Services** — three rows reusing current copy; Atelier numbers them as `Fig. 01–03`
7. **Section: Case study** — sample card / pull-quote; Atelier features `Confluence · III` as a banner above the article
8. **Signature** — footer-style monogram + contact block
9. **Switcher** — sticky top bar that flips between Current / Salon / Atelier without leaving the page

The `/styleguide/compare` page renders all three in a 3-column grid, with Current pinned in column 1 as the anchor.

## How to decide between Salon and Atelier

Both share the same Bureau DNA — patrician restraint, EB Garamond, classification labels, monogram H. They differ on a single axis: **how loudly the heritage thesis is stated.**

- **Salon** keeps the heritage as a posture (typography, restraint, voice). The reader infers it.
- **Atelier** states it explicitly with visual evidence (engravings, captions, plate numbering). The reader sees it.

For LPs/IC members who already see Hyperactive as "obviously serious," Salon is enough. For LPs who need to be told this brand belongs in the conversation about long-term capital, Atelier earns its imagery.
